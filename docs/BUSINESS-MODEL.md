# Ice & Instinct - Business Model (the two-floor house)

> Built 2026-06-11 from the deep-research market study (NYC metro + New Jersey) and the owner's constraints: zero capital, both floors grow together, weekly cadence, start light then formalize. Owner = Teimuraz Benidze (Temo).

## The one idea

The brand name already splits the business in two. Do not fight it - build on it.

- **INSTINCT** = high mixology. The founder, personally. Bespoke evenings, omakase, the sommelier story. Premium, $650-$3,000+. This is the entire current website. Keep 100%, change nothing.
- **ICE** = bartenders and events on call. Birthdays, weddings, corporate, parties. A booking + dispatch layer the founder runs from his phone. This is the new cash engine.

One house, two floors. Instinct is the art that wins prestige, press, and planner referrals. Ice is the engine that produces volume and weekly cash. The prestige of Instinct makes Ice's bartenders look better than any commodity agency; the volume of Ice pays the bills while Instinct stays exclusive.

House line for clients: **"From a bartender for Saturday night to a private mixology evening - one studio."**

## The engine (Phase 1, today, zero capital): the spread

This is exactly the owner's model, named and made repeatable:

1. A client needs a bartender (birthday in NJ, 4 hours, ~40 guests). Ice quotes a flat price: **$450**.
2. Temo calls a vetted bartender from the bench: "Birthday in Montclair Saturday, 4 hours, $200, can you go?" Vasya says yes.
3. Vasya pours. Client pays $450. Vasya gets $200. **Temo keeps the $250 spread.**

No inventory. No payroll. No upfront money. The only cost is the phone call and the website that already exists. The asset is the **bench of bartenders** and the **brand that makes the phone ring.**

### Realistic Phase-1 numbers (researched NYC/NJ rates)

- Freelance event bartender pay: $35-$65/hr (you pay Vasya ~$160-$220 for a 4-hour gig).
- Market client price for a solo event bartender: $50-$100/hr, or a packaged flat.
- **Spread per gig: $160-$280.**
- At 3-5 Ice gigs/week: **$2,000-$5,000/month** from Ice alone, on top of whatever Instinct premium work the founder does that week.

This is the highest-margin, lowest-cost model in the entire research: zero capital, no employees, margin is pure brokerage.

## The one legal nut to crack (costs almost nothing)

Here is the catch hidden inside "I collect $450 and pay Vasya $200": when **you** collect the client's money and **you** pay the bartender, the law can treat Vasya as **your worker**, not an independent contractor. In New Jersey the strict ABC test (operative **Oct 1, 2026**) makes this hard to dodge, because pouring drinks IS Ice's core business (fails "Prong B"). NJ has already assessed $10.6M+ across 12,500+ misclassified workers. We do NOT want that.

Two clean ways to keep the $250 spread AND stay safe - both near-zero cost:

**Option A - Markup with paper (recommended for Phase 1).** Keep collecting and paying the spread, but make every bartender a genuine independent subcontractor:
- One-page subcontractor agreement each bartender signs (I draft it - free).
- They work for others too, set their own availability, bring their own tools where possible (they are genuinely independent).
- **Require the bartender to carry their own liability insurance, OR run the event under the venue's / host's insurance.** This is the single most important line: it means a drunk-guest accident is the bartender's problem and their insurer's problem, not a personal lawsuit against Temo. Many freelance bartenders already carry it; "must have own insurance" becomes a bench requirement.

**Option B - Transparent booking fee (zero risk, lower spread).** Vasya invoices the client directly for the pour ($300); Ice charges a separate, openly-stated **booking/matchmaking fee** ($120-$150). You never become the employer because you never pay the bartender - the client does. Spread is smaller but legal exposure is essentially nil.

**Plan:** start with Option A (bigger spread) + the insurance requirement + signed subcontractor paper. The day volume is real and steady, graduate the best 3-5 bartenders to **W2 employees** under the Ice brand, carry the agency's own GL + liquor-liability insurance, and add venues as additional insured (the market baseline). That is Phase 2.

## Pricing

### ICE floor - on-call bartenders & events (transparent, packaged, competes on convenience)

| Package | What | Client price | You pay bartender | Your spread |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| The Single | 1 bartender, up to 4h, up to ~50 guests | $450 | $180-$240 | ~$210-$270 |
| The Pair | 2 bartenders, up to 4h, 50-100 guests | $850 | $380-$440 | ~$410-$470 |
| The Wedding (Ice) | team, full event, scaled to guest count | $1,200-$2,400 | 45-55% of price | the rest |
| Add-ons | barback +$200, extra hour +$75/bartender, clear-ice upgrade +$X | pass-through + margin | | |

Rule of thumb: **client price = bartender pay x ~2.1** (solo packages; team/barback packages follow the $200-minimum spread rule instead), minimum spread $200/gig. Never quote below the bench cost + $200.

### INSTINCT floor - private mixology by the founder (unchanged)

$650 / $900 / $1,800 / $3,000+. Exactly today's tiers. Temo personally, with 1-2 helpers on the big ones. Strict prestige-price alignment - never discounted, never staffed out under this name. (Research: dropping the price-prestige link under the same luxury name moves the brand into "masstige" and destroys the luxury category. That is why Ice exists as a separate floor.)

## The website (the "Ice & Instinct panel" the owner mentioned)

Present two doors on the home and in nav:

- **INSTINCT** -> the existing premium world: founder, omakase, bespoke, my-story, the $650-$3,000+ tiers. No change.
- **ICE** -> a new `/events/` (or `/bartenders/`) landing: "Need a bartender this weekend? Book a vetted pro in NYC and New Jersey." Fast quote form, the packaged Ice pricing, the convenience promise (vetted, insured, fast). This is the dispatch storefront.
- **Join the bench** -> a `/work-with-us/` page where bartenders apply. This recruits the asset the whole engine depends on.

SEO bonus: Ice ranks for the huge-volume commodity searches ("bartender for hire NJ", "wedding bartender NYC") that Instinct can never rank for without cheapening itself; Instinct keeps owning the premium and "cocktail omakase" niche. Two nets, no overlap, same domain compounding authority.

## The bench (the real moat)

The business is only as strong as the bartenders Ice can dispatch. Build it deliberately:

- **Recruit:** a "join the bench" page + posts in NYC/NJ bartender groups + word of mouth. Target 10-20 vetted bartenders in Year 1.
- **Vet:** short trial / tasting before they go out under the name. Quality is the brand.
- **Tier:** A-bench (premium-capable, can flex up to Instinct-adjacent jobs) and B-bench (solid standard events). Match tier to job.
- **Require:** own liability insurance (or willingness to work under venue insurance), reliability, presentation.

Owner's job shifts over time from "the bartender" to "the man who runs the best bench in the metro."

---

## Weekly rhythm (the harmony the owner asked for - both floors at once)

- Founder personally: ~1 Instinct premium evening/week + floats onto ~1 Ice flagship as the lead.
- Ice dispatch: 2-4 freelancer gigs/week, run entirely from the phone.
- Every inbound lead (site form, Google profile, planner referral) gets triaged: a fit for the founder's hands -> Instinct; everything else -> Ice dispatch. Nothing is turned away; it just routes to the right floor.

This is the "sit, earn, occasionally go out for the premium jobs" life the owner described - made systematic.

## 5-year arc

- **Year 1:** Run the spread engine (Option A) + Instinct premium in parallel. Build the bench to 10-20. Prove the weekly cash. Near-zero cost.
- **Year 2:** Volume justifies structure. Form the LLC cleanly, graduate top bartenders to W2 under Ice, carry agency GL + liquor-liability insurance, land 2-3 recurring corporate accounts (predictable monthly Ice revenue). Two-brand legal separation formalized.
- **Years 3-5:** Ice is the cash machine (staffing/events brand, recurring corporate + wedding-season volume); Instinct is the prestige halo that wins press, planners, and the highest-margin private work. Optional: license the playbook (bench + brand + booking system) to a second metro.
